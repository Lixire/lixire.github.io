A well-formed formula is defined as:

  • a single atom (ex: p)
  • (\not alpha)
  • (alpha \wedge B), (alpha \vee beta), (alpha \rightarrow beta)
  • Where alpha, beta are well-formed formulae.
  • eg. (notp \wedge q) wouldn’t get full marks since notp is not in brackets
